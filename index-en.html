Interim leadership

There are moments in an organization when the problem is not the lack of strategy or information, but the inability to execute with clarity.

Decisions get delayed, priorities constantly shift, and what used to work no longer sustains the operation.

In those moments, adding more analysis rarely solves the problem. In many cases, it makes it worse.

What is needed is intervention.

Interim leadership is not a permanent function or an external recommendation. It is the ability to enter an organization, assume direct responsibility for what is happening, and restore order, clarity, and direction in a critical moment.

It is not about observing, but acting.

Knowing when to intervene is key. You can identify some of those signals in when an interim director is needed.

Understanding what that intervention involves is equally important, and is explained in what an interim director does.

And recognizing the errors that tend to worsen a situation allows for clearer action, as described in mistakes in an organizational crisis.
